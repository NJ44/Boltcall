# Lead Magnet Analysis Rubric — 5-Dimension Scoring

> Score any lead magnet on 5 dimensions (1-10 each, total 50). This rubric identifies what makes a lead magnet worth downloading, sharing, and referencing — and what makes it forgettable.

---

## Why This Rubric Exists

The best LinkedIn lead magnets do 3 things simultaneously:
1. **Deliver immediate value** (so the downloader trusts you more after reading)
2. **Represent a gap** (something unique you can fill with your own version)
3. **Signal authority** (the creator clearly knows more than average)

A score below 30/50 = weak. 30-40 = solid. 40+ = genuinely strong.

---

## Dimension 1: Hook Strength (1-10)

**Question:** Does the title and first page make you want to read the entire thing?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1-3 | Generic title ("The Ultimate Guide to..."), no hook on first page |
| 4-6 | Somewhat specific, creates mild interest, readable opening |
| 7-8 | Specific title with clear value prop, strong first page hook |
| 9-10 | Irresistible title that creates immediate urgency + curiosity, first page makes you commit |

**What to look for:**
- Title: Does it own a specific outcome? ("0 to 10K LinkedIn followers in 90 days" vs. "LinkedIn Growth Guide")
- Opening page: Is there a hook that creates a "why I'm the person to teach you this" moment?
- Promise: Is the value proposition clear and specific?

---

## Dimension 2: Value Density (1-10)

**Question:** How much actionable value is packed in per page?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1-3 | Mostly filler, vague advice, padded with definitions and obvious statements |
| 4-6 | Some actionable content but diluted by fluff |
| 7-8 | High ratio of actionable to filler, examples included, can apply immediately |
| 9-10 | Almost every page has a system, template, or specific tactic ready to use |

**What to look for:**
- Ratio of "here's what to do" vs. "here's why this matters" (should skew toward "what")
- Templates, frameworks, checklists, examples — not just explanations
- Specificity: "$47,000 in 30 days" beats "significant revenue"
- Page count vs. value delivered (a 3-page checklist can score higher than a 40-page ebook)

---

## Dimension 3: CTA Quality (1-10)

**Question:** Does the lead magnet convert readers into the next step (email, call, purchase)?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1-3 | No CTA, or CTA buried on the last page only |
| 4-6 | CTA present but generic ("Visit our website") or mismatched to content |
| 7-8 | Relevant CTA with clear next step, appears multiple times |
| 9-10 | CTAs are perfectly topic-coupled, appear naturally at the right moments, create desire for the next product |

**What to look for:**
- Does the CTA make sense given what was just taught? ("You now understand the system — want help implementing it? →")
- Is there a clear next step that requires no guessing?
- Multiple touchpoints (not just end-of-document)
- Does it drive to an email capture, calendar link, or product page?

---

## Dimension 4: Format & Design (1-10)

**Question:** Does the design make the content easier to consume and trust?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1-3 | Wall of text, no visual hierarchy, poor typography |
| 4-6 | Readable but forgettable, basic formatting |
| 7-8 | Clean layout, consistent branding, good use of headers and visual breaks |
| 9-10 | Distinctive brand design, high-contrast, uses tables/callouts/icons effectively, immediately identifiable |

**What to look for:**
- Is the font readable and the contrast high enough?
- Are headers, subheads, and callout boxes used to break up text?
- Does the design reflect brand authority (consistent colors, logo present)?
- Would a screenshot of any random page look professional?

---

## Dimension 5: Uniqueness / Moat (1-10)

**Question:** Does this lead magnet contain ideas, data, or frameworks that couldn't be found elsewhere?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1-3 | Everything could be found in a Google search or ChatGPT prompt |
| 4-6 | Has some original framing, but the ideas are largely recycled |
| 7-8 | Contains proprietary frameworks, personal data, or original research |
| 9-10 | Genuinely novel — insights, systems, or data that exist nowhere else |

**What to look for:**
- Does the creator share their actual process/system with specifics?
- Are there named frameworks (the "XYZ Method") that feel proprietary?
- Personal results or client case studies with real numbers?
- Counter-intuitive ideas that challenge conventional wisdom?

---

## Post-Scoring Analysis

After scoring, always answer:

1. **The 3 best ideas in this lead magnet** (ideas worth extracting for your own use)
2. **The biggest gap** (what it promises but doesn't fully deliver)
3. **What a 10/10 version of this would look like** (your opportunity)
4. **How to adapt the best idea for your brand/ICP**
