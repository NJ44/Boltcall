# DM Objection Handlers — 8 Pre-Written Replies

> Pre-written responses to the 8 most common objections in LinkedIn DM outreach. Each reply is designed to keep the conversation alive without being pushy, and to gather information that helps qualify or disqualify the lead.

---

## How to Use These

When a prospect replies with one of these objections:
1. Identify which objection type it is
2. Use the pre-written reply as a starting point
3. Personalize with 1 specific detail from their message
4. Aim to either (a) move to next step or (b) gather more qualifying information

**Rule:** Never argue with an objection. Acknowledge it, adjust, and ask a single question.

---

## Objection 1: "Not Interested Right Now"

**What it usually means:** Timing is genuinely bad, or they haven't seen enough value yet.

```
Reply:
Totally fair, [Name] — timing matters more than most people admit.

Can I ask: is it more that this isn't a priority right now, or that the right moment hasn't come up yet?

Either way, happy to reconnect in [specific month]. I'll follow up then.
```

**Goal:** Distinguish between "wrong time" and "wrong fit." If it's timing, set a follow-up reminder.

---

## Objection 2: "How Did You Find Me?"

**What it usually means:** They're cautious or curious. Not necessarily a rejection — often just a request for context.

```
Reply:
[Name], your post on [specific topic] came up in my feed last week — [one specific thing they said or point they made that stuck with me].

That's the reason I reached out. Seemed like someone worth connecting with.
```

**Goal:** Prove you're a real person who paid attention. Specificity is everything here.

---

## Objection 3: "What Are You Selling?"

**What it usually means:** They're skeptical of outreach and want to know your angle upfront.

```
Reply:
Nothing yet, [Name] — and I'm not going to pitch you without context.

I reached out because [genuine, specific reason — not a sales line].

If at some point there's a fit, we'll both know. But that's not why I messaged.
```

**Goal:** Disarm defensiveness with honesty. Don't try to sneak past this objection — address it directly.

---

## Objection 4: "Can You Send More Information?"

**What it usually means:** They're curious but not ready to commit to a call. This is a soft yes — handle it carefully.

```
Reply:
Of course — here's what's most relevant given what I know about your situation:

[Resource — specific link, case study, or 2-3 sentence tailored explanation]

Quick question to make sure I send the right thing: are you more focused on [option A] or [option B] right now?
```

**Goal:** Deliver value, then qualify. The question at the end narrows focus and keeps the conversation going.

---

## Objection 5: "I'll Think About It"

**What it usually means:** They're not saying no, but they don't have enough information or motivation to say yes yet.

```
Reply:
Of course, [Name] — no rush.

One question, if you don't mind: what would make this a clear yes for you?

Happy to address whatever's holding it up, or give you space if now just isn't the right time.
```

**Goal:** Surface the real objection hidden behind "I'll think about it." Most people won't volunteer what they need — you have to ask.

---

## Objection 6: "I'm Too Busy Right Now"

**What it usually means:** Genuine time constraint, or they don't see enough value to make room.

```
Reply:
I get it — no shortage of that.

I'll check back in in [specific timeframe: "a few weeks" / "early April"].

One quick question before I do: [key qualifying question that moves your understanding forward — e.g. "Is [topic] actually a priority for Q2, or has it shifted?"]
```

**Goal:** Respect the constraint while gathering information about their actual priorities. The question also shows you're thinking about their situation, not just following a script.

---

## Objection 7: "We Already Have Someone for That"

**What it usually means:** They've solved the problem you're hinting at (or think they have).

```
Reply:
Good to know — always helpful to understand what's already in place.

Out of curiosity, what's working well with that setup? And is there anything that's still a friction point?

Not looking to displace anything — just trying to understand the full picture.
```

**Goal:** Gather intelligence about their current solution. Often, "we already have someone" means they have a partial solution with gaps you could fill. Find the gap without being adversarial.

---

## Objection 8: "What's the Price?"

**What it usually means:** They're interested enough to think about money, which is a positive signal. But asking for price before context = premature conversation.

```
Reply:
[Name], price depends heavily on your situation — there's no single number that applies to everyone we work with.

Before I give you a number that might be wrong for your context, can I ask: what outcome are you actually trying to achieve with [relevant topic]?

That'll tell me much more about what would make sense.
```

**Goal:** Redirect from price to value. You can't give a meaningful number without understanding their situation — and understanding their situation qualifies them as a lead.

---

## Universal Objection Handling Principles

| Principle | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Acknowledge before responding | "I get it" / "Totally fair" / "That makes sense" — then respond |
| One question per reply | More than one question = pressure. One question = curiosity. |
| Never argue | Arguing with an objection makes you look defensive. Agree where you can. |
| Ask to understand, not to overcome | The goal is information, not to "win" the objection |
| Give a specific follow-up timeframe | "I'll check back" = weak. "I'll reach out in early April" = trustworthy |
| Know when to stop | If they say no twice, send a graceful exit and move on |

---

## Graceful Exit Template

If after 2-3 follow-ups the conversation isn't moving:

```
[Name], I don't want to keep pulling at something that isn't the right fit right now.

I'll bow out here — but if [topic/situation] ever shifts, feel free to reach back out.

Either way, I'll keep an eye out for your posts. Always interesting.
```

**Never:** End with "please let me know if anything changes." That puts the work on them. End with something that keeps you in their mind positively.
