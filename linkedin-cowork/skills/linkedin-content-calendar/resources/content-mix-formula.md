# Content Mix Formula — Format Rotation & CTA Cycling

> The content mix formula prevents two of the biggest LinkedIn growth killers: format monotony (posting the same type every day) and CTA fatigue (always pushing the same offer). This formula keeps both varied while maintaining strategic direction.

---

## Why Format Rotation Matters

If you post only one format (e.g., only text posts), the algorithm sees no variety and the audience mentally tunes you out. Different formats reach different segments of your audience:

- **Carousel viewers** = people who want to learn (save-first behavior)
- **Story readers** = people who want to relate (comment-first behavior)
- **List readers** = people who want to do (share-first behavior)
- **Image viewers** = people who browse fast (screenshot-first behavior)

Rotation ensures you're reaching all 4 segments every 2 weeks.

---

## The 4-Format Rotation (Week A → Week B)

### Week A
| Day | Format |
|-----|--------|
| Mon | Hook Stack |
| Tue | Long-Form List |
| Wed | Personal Story |
| Thu | Carousel |
| Fri | Repurpose or Hot Take |

### Week B
| Day | Format |
|-----|--------|
| Mon | Long-Form Image |
| Tue | Deep-Work System Reveal |
| Wed | Repurpose |
| Thu | Hook Stack |
| Fri | Personal Story |

**Pattern:** The 4 core formats (List, Carousel, Hook Stack, Story) each appear across 2 weeks. No format repeats on the same day. Peak days (Tue, Thu) always get high-reach formats.

---

## CTA Rotation — 2-Week Cycle

Each 2-week block alternates between two CTA themes:

| Week | CTA Theme | Applied to All Posts |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Week 1 | Newsletter | Every post → drive to newsletter subscription |
| Week 2 | Lead Magnet | Every post → drive to free resource download |

**Why alternate instead of always the same?**
- Audience fatigue: seeing "join my newsletter" on every post → readers start to ignore the CTA
- Different posts reach different audience segments who may prefer different entry points
- The lead magnet CTA creates urgency and novelty every other week

## Content Category Rotation

Beyond format and CTA, rotate through 3-4 content categories across the 2-week plan.

**Goal:** Every category appears at least once per week, and no category appears more than twice in the same week.

### How to assign categories to slots:

```
Define 3-4 content categories from brand-identity-template.md
Example: [Systems] | [Revenue] | [AI & Tools] | [Mindset]

Week 1 example:
Mon (Hook Stack) → Systems
Tue (List) → Revenue
Wed (Story) → Mindset
Thu (Carousel) → AI & Tools
Fri (Repurpose) → Systems (repurposing an existing systems post)

Week 2 example:
Mon (Image) → Revenue
Tue (System Reveal) → Systems
Wed (Repurpose) → AI & Tools
Thu (Hook Stack) → Mindset
Fri (Story) → Revenue
```

**Rule:** Never have the same category 3 days in a row. The reader should feel like they're getting breadth, not a single topic drummed into them.

---

## The 2-Promotional-Posts Rule

In any 2-week calendar, at most **2 posts** should directly mention a product or offer by name.

The remaining 8 posts build authority, trust, and audience — which is what makes those 2 promotional posts convert.

**Promotional post definition:** Any post that names your product, service, or paid offer and asks the reader to buy or book.

**Not promotional:** CTAs to newsletter, lead magnet, or free resources — these are part of every post and don't count toward the 2.

## Hook-to-Category Coupling

The hook and content category should feel natural together. Some hook types work better with certain categories:

| Hook Type | Best Content Categories |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Curiosity Gap | Systems, AI & Tools, Behind-the-scenes |
| Data + Identity | Revenue, Growth, Research-heavy topics |
| Story Open | Mindset, Leadership, Hiring |
| Result First | Revenue, Systems, Client results |
| Contrarian | Any — strongest with topics that have conventional wisdom to challenge |
| System Reveal | Systems, AI workflows, Productivity |

## Applying Theme or Launch Context

If the user provides a theme or launch context (e.g., "I'm launching a new course next week"):

**Rule:** Adjust 1-2 hook ideas and CTAs to support the launch without making every post promotional.

**Example with theme "launching LinkedIn course":**

- Mon Hook Stack: "7 mistakes I made in my first year on LinkedIn" (authority builder → no mention of course)
- Tue List: "The LinkedIn posting system I used to hit 10K followers" (authority builder → no mention of course)
- Wed Story: "The post that changed my LinkedIn trajectory" (relationship building → no mention of course)
- Thu Carousel: "My exact LinkedIn content calendar [free inside]" → CTA: "The full system is in my new course → [link in comments]" ← **PROMOTIONAL POST 1**
- Fri Repurpose: repurpose best past content (no promotion)

Week 2:
- Mon Image: dense value image about LinkedIn algorithm (authority)
- Tue System Reveal: "The 30-min content workflow I use every week" (authority)
- Wed Repurpose: repurpose a top-performing post (no promotion)
- Thu Hook Stack: "Why I'm betting everything on LinkedIn in 2026" → CTA: "Join the course before seats close → [link in comments]" ← **PROMOTIONAL POST 2**
- Fri Story: "The real story behind why I built this course" (relationship building)

**Result:** 2 promotional posts, 8 authority builders, 1 consistent launch narrative.
