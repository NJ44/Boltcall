# Posting Schedule — Optimal Days, Times & Format-to-Day Mapping

> The posting schedule is not a preference — it's a reach multiplier. The same post published at the wrong time can get 3x fewer impressions than when posted at the right time.

---

## Weekly Posting Map

| Day | Engagement Level | Best Format | Why |
|-----|-----------------|-------------|-----|
| Monday | Medium | Hook Stack | Warm-up the week — lower stakes, gets your name in feeds |
| Tuesday | PEAK | Long-Form List | Highest reach format on highest engagement day |
| Wednesday | High | Personal Story | Mid-week relationship building — people have time to read |
| Thursday | PEAK | Carousel / System Reveal | Second peak day — use second-highest reach format |
| Friday | Medium-Low | Repurpose or Hot Take | End of week — clean out the content queue |
| Saturday | Low | Skip or Personal Story | Only post if the story is unusually strong |
| Sunday | Low | Skip | LinkedIn audience is not on Sunday |

---

## Optimal Posting Times

**Always:** 8:00am OR 12:00pm (noon) in your audience's primary timezone

**Why 8am:** Catches the morning LinkedIn scroll — people check LinkedIn before the workday starts

**Why 12pm:** Catches the lunch break scroll — second peak window of the day

**Never post between 9am-11am:** Most B2B professionals are in meetings. Posting at 9am means your post appears in feed when nobody is there to engage → low velocity in the 45-minute window → limited algorithmic distribution.

**Never post after 4pm on weekdays:** Engagement drops sharply as the workday ends.

---

## Weekly Minimum vs. Optimal

| Mode | Frequency | Days |
|------|-----------|------|
| Maintenance | 3x/week | Tue, Wed, Thu |
| Growth | 5x/week | Mon-Fri |
| Aggressive growth | 7x/week | Every day |

**Note:** LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistent posting. Going from 0 posts/week to 5 posts/week will give a bigger boost than going from 5 to 7. Consistency beats volume every time.

## The 45-Minute Rule (Applies to All Days)

After posting at 8am or 12pm:
1. Set a 45-minute timer
2. Reply to every comment that comes in during this window
3. At the 45-minute mark, add your link to the first comment (if your CTA has a URL)
4. OR: edit the post to add the URL at the 45-minute mark

This maximizes algorithmic reach while still capturing CTA clicks.

## Manual Posting Only

**Never use third-party scheduling tools** (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, etc.) to post directly to LinkedIn.

LinkedIn's algorithm detects API-published posts and reduces initial distribution compared to natively-posted content.

**What you can do with Buffer/Notion:**
- Draft posts and queue them for review
- Use them as a content management system
- Copy-paste to LinkedIn at posting time

**What you cannot do:**
- Auto-publish via Buffer directly to LinkedIn

---

## Time Zone Strategy

If your ICP is in multiple time zones, post in the time zone where the majority of your ICP is located.

For B2B US-focused content: Eastern Time (ET) — this is where the largest concentration of B2B decision-makers is active on LinkedIn.

For global audiences: 8am ET catches EU afternoon + US morning overlap.
