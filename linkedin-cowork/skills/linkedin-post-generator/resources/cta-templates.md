# CTA Templates — Call-to-Action Patterns + Timing Rules

> The CTA block is the last thing readers see. It has 3 jobs: get them to repost, get them to follow, and get them to take one next step. Each post needs all 3, in that order.

---

## The 3-Part CTA Formula

Every LinkedIn post CTA has exactly 3 parts:

```
♻️ [Repost prompt]
👉 [Follow prompt]
💡 [Lead magnet / next step — topic-coupled]
```

---

## Part 1: Repost Prompt

**Goal:** Trigger algorithm amplification. Reposts are the #1 signal LinkedIn uses to expand reach.

### Rules:
- Never say "please share" — too passive
- Never say "if you found this valuable" — too vague
- Name the identity group who should repost — makes it specific

### Templates:
```
♻️ Repost if this helped a fellow [identity group].
♻️ Repost this for any [role/identity] in your network.
♻️ Repost if you've seen this mistake before.
♻️ Repost to save this for later.
♻️ Repost if this challenges the way you think about [topic].
♻️ Know a [role] who needs to see this? Repost it.
```

---

## Part 2: Follow Prompt

**Goal:** Convert post readers into followers.

### Rules:
- Always include the handle (@)
- Always pair with the niche one-liner — not just "for more content"
- Keep it to one line

### Templates:
```
👉 Follow @[handle] for [niche one-liner].
👉 Follow @[handle] — I post about [niche topic] [frequency].
👉 Follow @[handle] if you want [specific outcome] without [common frustration].
👉 @[handle] for [niche] every [Tue/Thu/day].
```

---

## Part 3: Lead Magnet / Next Step (Topic-Coupled)

**Goal:** Drive one specific action that's directly related to what the post was about.

### The Topic-Coupling Rule
The lead magnet CTA must be related to the post topic. Never use a generic "join my newsletter" CTA.

**Bad (generic):**
```
💡 Join my newsletter for weekly tips.
```

**Good (topic-coupled):**
```
💡 Want the full system I use for this? It's in my newsletter — boltcall.com/newsletter
💡 I built a free [topic] checklist. Grab it at [URL]
💡 Going deeper on [topic] this week in The Operator — subscribe free at [URL]
```

### CTA by Week (Calendar Rotation)
- **Week 1 CTA theme = Newsletter**: Drive people to subscribe to your newsletter
- **Week 2 CTA theme = Lead Magnet**: Drive people to download/access your free resource

### Newsletter CTA Templates (Week 1):
```
💡 I cover [niche topic] every [day] in [Newsletter Name]. Free at [URL]
💡 This is 10% of what I share in [Newsletter Name]. Subscribe free → [URL]
💡 If this post helped, the newsletter goes 10x deeper → [URL]
💡 Join [N]+ [identity group] reading [Newsletter Name]: [URL]
```

### Lead Magnet CTA Templates (Week 2):
```
💡 I made a free [resource type] on exactly this. Get it at [URL]
💡 Want the [template/checklist/guide]? It's free → [URL]
💡 I packaged this into a [N]-page [guide/system/framework]. Free download: [URL]
💡 [Resource name] — free for [identity group]: [URL]
```

---

## Link Timing Rule

**Never put links in the post body.** LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes posts with external links, reducing reach by approximately 60%.

**The 45-Minute Rule:**
1. Post without any links
2. Monitor engagement for 45 minutes
3. When engagement spikes (or at the 45-minute mark), edit the post to add the link
4. OR: Add the link in the first comment, then like your own comment to pin it

This preserves full algorithmic reach during the critical early window while still capturing the CTA click.

## Full CTA Block Example

```
♻️ Repost if this helps any founders in your network.
👉 Follow @boltcall for LinkedIn systems that actually drive revenue.
💡 I built a free 5-day LinkedIn launch system. Get it free at boltcall.com/linkedin
```

## CTA Don'ts

| Don't do this | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| "Like and comment for more" | Beg tactics suppress reach |
| "Drop a 🔥 if you agree" | Reaction-bait is penalized |
| "What do you think?" (generic) | No context = no engagement |
| "Share with someone who needs this" | Vague — names the action, not the who |
| Link in post body | Algorithm penalty ~60% reach reduction |
| Two links in same CTA | Dilutes the action — one CTA per post |
