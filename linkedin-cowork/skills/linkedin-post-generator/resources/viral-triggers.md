# Viral Triggers — 8 Shareability Signals

> Every post should have at least 2 viral triggers engineered in. These are the psychological and social mechanisms that cause someone to repost, save, or send a post to a colleague. Reposts are the #1 algorithm signal on LinkedIn.

---

## Why Viral Triggers Matter

LinkedIn reach compounds when a post gets reposts, not just reactions. A like keeps the post in one network. A repost exposes it to the reposter's entire follower base.

The question to ask for every post: **"What would make someone send this to their team in Slack right now?"**

If you can't answer that, the post doesn't have enough trigger.

---

## The 8 Viral Triggers

### 1. Identity Reinforcement
**What it is:** The post makes the reader feel seen, smart, or validated for believing what they already believe.
**Why it spreads:** People share content that represents who they are.

**How to engineer it:**
- Name a specific identity: "If you're a B2B founder..." / "The best operators I know..."
- Validate a belief they hold but rarely see articulated: "Deep work isn't about hours. It's about leverage."
- Make them feel ahead-of-the-curve: "Most people won't see this for another year."

**Signal words:** "If you're...", "The smartest [X] I know...", "Only [identity] understand this..."

---

### 2. Social Currency
**What it is:** Sharing the post makes the reader look knowledgeable, current, or insightful to their network.
**Why it spreads:** People share content to signal their taste and expertise.

**How to engineer it:**
- Include data or insights most people don't know yet
- Frame it as "here's what nobody is talking about"
- Make the repost a status signal: "I'm sharing this because I know it"

**Signal words:** "Here's what most people don't know...", "The data is clear...", "Most [X] are still using [old method]..."

---

### 3. Practical Value
**What it is:** The post is immediately useful — someone can take action today.
**Why it spreads:** People forward useful resources to colleagues ("You need to see this").

**How to engineer it:**
- Include a specific framework, checklist, or template
- Make the takeaway something someone can try this week
- Compress a system into numbered steps they can follow

**Signal words:** "Here's the exact system...", "3 things to do this week...", "Step 1 of the framework..."

---

### 4. Emotional Resonance
**What it is:** The post triggers a real emotional response — vulnerability, struggle, triumph, or recognition.
**Why it spreads:** Emotion is the primary driver of sharing on every platform.

**How to engineer it:**
- Share a real failure or low point before the lesson
- Name the emotion explicitly: "I was embarrassed", "I was terrified"
- Mirror an experience the reader has had: "You know that feeling when..."

**Signal words:** "I almost gave up...", "I was embarrassed to admit...", "If you've ever felt like..."

---

### 5. Data Surprise
**What it is:** A statistic or number that contradicts what most people assume.
**Why it spreads:** Surprising data triggers a "wait, what?" response that makes people want to share.

**How to engineer it:**
- Lead with the number first: "96% of LinkedIn posts get fewer than 1,000 impressions."
- Pair with a counter-intuitive implication
- Source it if you can (LinkedIn data, internal analysis, research)

**Signal words:** "[X]% of...", "After analyzing [N]...", "The real number is..."

---

### 6. Contrarian Take
**What it is:** The post directly challenges conventional wisdom — and makes a credible case.
**Why it spreads:** Both agreement AND disagreement drive engagement. Comments explode on contrarian posts.

**How to engineer it:**
- Name the popular belief explicitly: "Everyone says [X]."
- State your counter-claim directly: "They're wrong."
- Support it with evidence: data, personal results, or logical argument

**Warning:** The take must be credible, not just edgy. Contrarianism without substance looks desperate.

**Signal words:** "Everyone says...", "The conventional wisdom is wrong...", "Stop doing [popular thing]..."

---

### 7. Visual Density
**What it is:** An image or carousel slide so packed with value that people screenshot and save it.
**Why it spreads:** Screenshots become second-generation distribution — the image lives outside LinkedIn.

**How to engineer it:**
- Build an image that contains a complete framework (not just a quote)
- Use a 3×3 grid, a pyramid, or a comparison table — not bullet points
- Make sure it's readable as a screenshot without needing the post text

**Applied to:** Long-form Image format and Carousel format

---

### 8. Curiosity Gap
**What it is:** The hook withholds the payoff until after "see more" — forcing the click.
**Why it spreads:** The reader can't share something they haven't read — so they click through first, which boosts post signals.

**How to engineer it:**
- Set up the premise, but don't give the conclusion until line 4+
- Use phrases that promise resolution: "Here's what I found:", "This is what changed:"
- Never answer the question you're asking in the hook

**Signal words:** "Here's what happened:", "This is the part nobody talks about:", "Here's why:"

---

## Minimum Trigger Requirement

Every post must have at least **2 triggers** before publishing.

### Quick Checklist:
- [ ] Does this make the reader feel smart for reading it? (Identity Reinforcement)
- [ ] Would someone send this to their team? (Practical Value)
- [ ] Does this include a number that surprises? (Data Surprise)
- [ ] Does this challenge something people believe? (Contrarian)
- [ ] Does this make someone feel understood? (Emotional Resonance)
- [ ] Does this image or structure deserve to be screenshot? (Visual Density)
- [ ] Does the hook force a "see more" click? (Curiosity Gap)
- [ ] Would sharing this make the reader look smart? (Social Currency)

If fewer than 2 boxes are checked, rewrite the hook or add a data point before posting.

## Trigger Combinations by Goal

| Goal | Best trigger combo |
|------|------------------|
| Maximum reach | Data Surprise + Contrarian |
| Maximum saves | Practical Value + Visual Density |
| Maximum comments | Contrarian + Emotional Resonance |
| Maximum reposts | Identity Reinforcement + Social Currency |
| Maximum follower conversion | Identity Reinforcement + Practical Value |
