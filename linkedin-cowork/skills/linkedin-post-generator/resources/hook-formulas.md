# Hook Formulas — 12 Proven Patterns

> The hook is the first 3 lines of any LinkedIn post. It's the only thing that determines if someone clicks "see more" or scrolls away. These are the 12 formats that consistently perform.

---

## The Rules (apply to all hooks)

- **≤225 characters** — this is approximately 3 short lines
- **Line 1** = stop the scroll (curiosity, data, or identity)
- **Line 2** = deepen the hook (identity signal, data, or contradiction)
- **Line 3** = promise value or force a click ("Here's why:", "The truth:", "What I learned:")
- **Language**: 5th-grade reading level. Short sentences. No jargon.
- **"I" / "I've"** appears in 67% of top-performing posts — use first person
- **Specificity beats vagueness** — "3 years" beats "years", "$47,000" beats "a lot"
- **Never start with "I am..."** — start with the situation, result, or observation

## The 12 Hook Formulas

### 1. Curiosity Gap
Create information asymmetry. Reader knows something interesting exists, but not what it is yet.

```
Template:
I just [did/read/discovered] something I can't stop thinking about.
[One-sentence setup that creates tension]
[Promise: "Here's what it taught me:" / "This changes everything:"]

Example:
I just ran a test I didn't expect to work.
Posted the same idea 3 different ways on the same day.
The results were absurd. Here's what happened:
```

---

### 2. Data + Identity
Lead with a number that surprises, then call out a specific identity group.

```
Template:
[Specific number or percentage]. [Surprising implication].
[Identity group] [are doing X / are missing Y / don't know Z].
[Promise: "Here's how to be different:"]

Example:
96% of LinkedIn posts get fewer than 1,000 impressions.
B2B founders are writing essays nobody reads.
Here's what actually gets reach:
```

---

### 3. Contrarian
Directly challenge the most common advice in your niche.

```
Template:
Everyone says [X]. They're wrong.
[Your counter-claim in one sentence]
Here's what actually works:

Example:
Everyone says post every day. They're wrong.
Posting 3x/week with a system beats 7x/week without one.
Here's the difference:
```

---

### 4. Story Open
Start at a low point or before/after moment. Don't build up to the drama — start inside it.

```
Template:
[X] years ago I [low point or situation].
Today [dramatic contrast — high point or change].
Here's what changed:

Example:
2 years ago I had 340 LinkedIn followers.
Today that number is 47,000.
Here's the exact thing that changed:
```

---

### 5. List Tease
Announce a list with a specific number and a compelling category name.

```
Template:
[Number] things nobody tells you about [topic]:
(I learned these the hard way.)
[Blank line — then start the list]

Example:
9 LinkedIn rules that took me 3 years to learn:
(None of them are what the gurus teach.)
```

---

### 6. Personal Confession
Admit a mistake or misconception. Vulnerability creates trust and engagement.

```
Template:
I used to [common mistake or wrong belief].
Then I [event that changed my thinking].
I've never gone back. Here's why:

Example:
I used to write long, thoughtful LinkedIn posts every day.
Then I posted a 3-line hook stack that got 200,000 impressions.
I've never gone back. Here's why:
```

---

### 7. Question Hook
Ask a question that forces the reader to answer in their head — the answer being "yes" or "that's me."

```
Template:
What if [challenge to conventional assumption]?
[One sentence grounding it in reality]
[Promise or teaser]

Example:
What if the reason your posts aren't getting traction has nothing to do with the content?
After analyzing 500+ LinkedIn posts, I found the real pattern.
It's not what you think:
```

---

### 8. Result First
Lead with the outcome, then reverse-engineer the method.

```
Template:
I [achieved specific result] in [specific timeframe].
[One-sentence "without X" or "while Y" that makes it more impressive]
Here's the exact system:

Example:
I generated $180,000 in pipeline from one LinkedIn carousel.
No ads. No DMs. No sales calls.
Here's the exact structure I used:
```

---

### 9. Identity Call-Out
Name a specific identity your ICP holds and speak directly to them.

```
Template:
If you're a [specific identity], this will change how you think about [topic].
[One-sentence setup]
[Promise]

Example:
If you're a B2B founder posting on LinkedIn, this will change how you think about hooks.
Most of what you've been told about writing for reach is wrong.
Here's the data:
```

---

### 10. Myth-Buster
Name a myth explicitly, then promise to kill it with evidence.

```
Template:
The [most common advice about topic] is completely wrong.
[One-sentence counter-claim with credibility signal]
Here's the data that proves it:

Example:
The "post every day" advice is completely wrong.
I've tracked 200 accounts — daily posting without strategy actively hurts reach.
Here's the data that proves it:
```

---

### 11. Urgency + Platform Shift
Signal that something is changing right now that most people don't see.

```
Template:
[Platform/industry] is changing right now.
Most [identity group] don't see it yet.
Here's what I'm watching:

Example:
LinkedIn is changing right now.
Most B2B creators are still using 2022 tactics.
Here's what's actually working in 2026:
```

---

### 12. Prediction
Make a bold, specific prediction. Works especially well in fast-moving niches.

```
Template:
In [timeframe], [bold, specific prediction].
[One sentence of evidence or reasoning]
Here's why I'm betting on it:

Example:
In 12 months, AI-generated content will be invisible on LinkedIn.
The algorithm is already training to suppress it.
Here's why I'm betting on it — and what I'm doing instead:
```

## Hook Selection Guide

| Goal | Best Hook Types |
|------|----------------|
| Maximize reach / impressions | #2 (Data+Identity), #5 (List Tease), #8 (Result First) |
| Drive engagement / comments | #3 (Contrarian), #7 (Question), #10 (Myth-Buster) |
| Build trust / authority | #4 (Story), #6 (Confession), #8 (Result First) |
| Convert to followers | #9 (Identity Call-Out), #11 (Urgency), #1 (Curiosity Gap) |
| Viral / repost potential | #2 (Data), #3 (Contrarian), #12 (Prediction) |
