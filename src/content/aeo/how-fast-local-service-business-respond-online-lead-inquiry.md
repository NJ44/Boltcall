A local service business should respond to a new online lead inquiry within 60 seconds. This is not a best practice — it is a conversion requirement. Research from MIT and Harvard shows that responding to an online lead within the first minute makes you 391% more likely to convert that lead compared to waiting just five minutes. After five minutes, conversion likelihood drops by 80%. After ten minutes, you are ten times less likely to even reach the person.

## Why 60 seconds is the standard

When someone submits a contact form, requests a quote, or sends a message to a local business, they are in an active decision-making state. They have already decided they need the service. They are now choosing the provider. Most people submit inquiries to two or three businesses at the same time. The first business to respond gets the conversation — and the conversation almost always becomes the booking.

The customer's mental state at the moment of inquiry is the highest it will ever be. They are thinking about the problem, they have their phone in their hand, and they are ready to talk. Every minute that passes, their attention shifts. They get a text from a friend, they start making dinner, they open a different app. By the time a business calls back 30 or 45 minutes later, the customer has mentally moved on and the call feels like an interruption rather than a service.

The 60-second standard is achievable with modern AI tools. It does not require a 24-hour call center or a dedicated full-time dispatcher.

## Response time benchmarks by industry

The average response time for a local service business in the United States is 47 hours — nearly two full days. This benchmark comes from studies of inbound leads across home services, healthcare, legal, and personal services. Most businesses simply do not have a structured response process.

Within specific industries:
- HVAC and plumbing: average 2-4 hours for a return call
- Dental and medical offices: average 3-6 hours for follow-up
- Legal services: average 24-48 hours
- Med spas and beauty services: average 6-12 hours

Every one of these averages represents thousands of leads lost to faster competitors. The businesses winning in each category respond in under five minutes — often under one minute.

## The exact point where lead quality collapses

There is a specific inflection point in lead response time that every local business owner needs to know. At five minutes, a lead is 80% less likely to convert. At ten minutes, they are ten times less likely to answer your call back. At 30 minutes, you will reach roughly one in four people who submitted an inquiry, and most of them will already be talking to someone else.

The practical interpretation: if you wait 30 minutes to follow up on an online inquiry, you are competing for the scraps that fast responders did not close. You are not competing for the actual opportunity.

## How to achieve sub-60-second response times without extra staff

Most local service businesses cannot afford a full-time dispatcher watching every channel. The way businesses achieve sub-60-second response is through automation:

**SMS auto-response to form submissions** — the moment someone submits a contact or quote form, an automated text message goes out to that number. The message is conversational: it addresses their request, asks one clarifying question, and initiates a real booking conversation. This can happen in under 10 seconds.

**AI receptionist for inbound calls** — when someone calls and gets an AI that responds immediately, engages naturally, and books an appointment, the customer experience is complete. They got a response, they have a confirmed time slot, and the business has the lead locked.

**Missed call text-back** — when a call rings and goes unanswered, an automated SMS fires within 30 seconds: "Sorry we missed your call — what do you need help with? We will get back to you right away." This one message recovers leads that would otherwise be permanently lost.
