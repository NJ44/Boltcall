# How can a chiropractor automatically follow up with patients who called and got voicemail?

A chiropractor can automatically follow up with patients who reached voicemail by connecting an AI-powered missed-call text-back system to their practice phone line. When a call goes unanswered, the system immediately sends an SMS to the caller's number — typically within 30 to 60 seconds — acknowledging the missed call and inviting them to reply, book an appointment, or call back at their convenience.

## Why voicemail alone loses new chiropractic patients

Voicemail has a structural flaw: most callers do not leave messages. Industry data consistently shows that over 75% of callers who reach voicemail hang up without leaving a message. For chiropractic practices — where new patient calls represent significant revenue — this means the majority of missed calls are lost entirely unless the business has a system to recover them.

New patients in pain are motivated to book quickly. They typically call two or three chiropractors from their Google search results and book with whoever responds first. A practice that answers or sends an immediate text after a missed call still has a chance to win that patient. A practice that waits until the next morning to check voicemail almost certainly does not.

## How automatic missed-call follow-up works for chiropractic offices

The most effective approach for chiropractic practices involves two layers:

**Layer 1: Immediate SMS text-back**
When a call is missed, the system automatically sends a text message to the caller within 30 to 60 seconds. The message acknowledges the call, introduces the practice by name, and provides a direct way to book. For example: "Hi, this is Dr. [Name]'s office — we just missed your call. Would you like to book an appointment? Reply YES or click here to choose a time."

**Layer 2: AI voice or chat follow-up**
If the caller does not respond to the SMS within a set window (typically 10 to 30 minutes), the system can initiate a follow-up call using an AI voice agent. The AI calls the patient back, introduces itself as calling on behalf of the practice, and offers to book them immediately. This two-step approach recovers a significant percentage of missed calls that the text alone does not convert.

## The revenue case for chiropractic missed-call recovery

A new chiropractic patient entering a care plan is worth $800 to $2,500 in revenue over 3 to 6 months, plus ongoing maintenance care and referrals. For a practice that receives 15 new patient calls per week and misses 5 of them, recovering even 2 of those 5 calls per week through automatic follow-up represents 8 additional new patients per month.

At an average care plan value of $1,200, that is $9,600 in monthly revenue directly attributable to the missed-call recovery system — revenue that was previously walking out the door with every unanswered call.

## What information to include in the automatic follow-up message

Effective chiropractic missed-call follow-up messages include:
- Practice name (so the caller knows who is reaching out)
- Acknowledgment of the missed call (without making the patient feel ignored)
- A clear, low-friction action: reply to book, click a booking link, or respond with their availability
- Practice hours and an invitation to call back if they prefer a phone conversation

Avoid overly promotional language in the initial message. The goal is recovery, not sales. The patient already wanted to book — the message simply reopens the door.
